This tab analyses 190,000+ one-minute BTC price bars to find repeating patterns. Every bar is labelled UP (↑), DOWN (↓), or FLAT (=) based on whether price moved up, down, or stayed the same that minute. We then look at what sequences of bars tend to appear before a move in either direction.

Key finding: BTC has a persistent downward micro-bias — after any sequence of flat bars, price breaks down ~1.2× more often than it breaks up. The Compression Stats table shows this clearly: after 8 flat bars in a row, there's only a 10% chance of going up vs 12% chance of going down. The other 78% of the time it just stays flat. This is why the sniper only fires when volume spikes confirm a real move.

Signals & Horizons — What Is a Signal?
A signal is generated when BTC sits flat for 8+ consecutive minutes and then breaks out with a significant price move accompanied by elevated volume. The system places a LONG (betting price goes up) or SHORT (betting price goes down) based on the direction of the breakout.

The hit rate measures how often the signal was correct at various time horizons — e.g. H3 means "was the call right 3 minutes later?", H10 means "10 minutes later". A HIT means price moved in the predicted direction, a MISS means it moved the other way, a TIE means price was flat.

Key finding: SHORT signals dramatically outperform LONG signals (49.2% vs 29.2% hit rate at H10). This aligns with BTC's persistent downward micro-bias shown on the Pattern tab. The BTC Horizon Forecast shows the model's directional probability for the next 1 minute through 24 hours.

Model Explainer — How Does the Engine Work?
The RRP TECH engine runs a fully automated 5-step pipeline once a day. Here's what each step does in plain English:

1. Data Collection — Downloads every 1-minute BTC price candle from Binance. Currently has 190,000+ candles going back to November 2025.
2. BTC Regime Engine — Scores 6 macro signals (ETH/BTC ratio, 3-day momentum, order book imbalance, Fear & Greed index, hashrate change, USDT flows) each on a −1/0/+1 scale. Total score determines if we're in a Bull, Bear, or Neutral regime. This acts as the master gate — if BTC is bearish, no buys are made.
3. BTC Prediction Engine — A Random Forest (an ensemble of 800 decision trees) trained on 89 technical features predicts BTC's direction at 7 different time horizons (1 min to 24 hours).
4. Sniper Scanner — Ranks the top 15 liquid coins by a composite momentum + volume score called TopMoverScore. Each coin gets a direction label (UP/NOUP) from a second logistic regression model.
5. Alpha Dashboard — Combines regime + model confidence to assign an allocation tier (STRONG_BUY → SKIP) and a Kelly Criterion position size (a mathematically optimal bet-sizing formula that risks more when confidence is higher).

AUC = Area Under Curve — the model's ability to distinguish UP from DOWN coins. 0.5 = coin flip, 1.0 = perfect. Brier Score = how calibrated the probabilities are — lower is better. Val Recall = out of all the actual UP coins, how many did the model correctly identify.

BTC Regime Engine
Scores 6 macro signals on a −1/0/+1 scale. Score ≥ +2 → Bullish, ≤ −2 → Bearish, else Neutral. Uses logistic sigmoid: prob_up = 1 / (1 + e^(−0.35 × score)).

Signals: ETH/BTC ratio, 3D BTC momentum, Coinbase orderbook imbalance (top 50), Fear & Greed (contrarian: ≤30 = +1), hashrate change, USDT market cap flow. Today only Fear & Greed scored +1 (value=8, extreme fear). Confidence = 16.7%.
BTC Prediction Engine — RandomForest Multi-Horizon
Trains 7 separate RandomForest models (1m/5m/15m/30m/1h/4h/24h), each predicting whether price will be higher at horizon N. 89 features from 1m OHLCV: rolling returns, up/down/flat ratios, streak lengths, volatility windows, volume ratios, OHLC spreads.

800 trees, max_features=sqrt, min_samples_leaf=25. Consensus is a weighted vote (24h weight=2.0). Agreement threshold = 60%.

Acorn Model Health — Is the AI Getting Better?
The sniper model (a Logistic Regression classifier) retrains itself every time the pipeline runs, adding the latest batch of trades to its training data. This tab tracks whether the model is actually improving over time.

AUC (Area Under Curve) is the headline accuracy metric — think of it as a score from 0 to 1 measuring how well the model separates winning coins from losing ones. Below 0.5 = worse than random, 0.5–0.65 = weak edge, above 0.65 = meaningful edge. The chart shows every training run so you can see the model's journey.

Brier Score measures probability calibration — when the model says "60% chance of going up", does it actually go up 60% of the time? Lower Brier = more trustworthy probabilities. This is the most consistently improving metric — down from 0.46 early February to 0.243 now.

Recall = out of all coins that actually went UP, what % did the model correctly flag? Precision = out of all the coins the model flagged as UP, what % actually went UP. High recall with lower precision means the model casts a wide net — it catches most winners but also some losers. Feature importance shows which signals the model is leaning on most — liquidity (median_minute_usd) now dominates, which may be a concern as the model grows.

Entry Patterns — When Is the Best Time to Trade?
This tab answers a simple but powerful question: does it matter what time of day or day of the week you enter a trade? The answer, based on 190,000+ one-minute BTC bars, is yes — significantly.

Hour of Day (UTC) — The heatmap shows average 60-minute returns for each hour. Green hours are historically profitable entry points (20:00–21:00 UTC is the best single window). Red hours are negative on average (2:00–5:00 UTC, 14:00 UTC on Thursdays).

DOW = Day of Week. Tuesday 15:00 UTC is the single best repeating weekly setup — 88.9% win rate across 18 Tuesdays in the dataset. Thursday 14:00 UTC is the worst slot (36.2% win rate — better to sit out or short).

Interval simulation tests what happens if you enter every N minutes and hold for H minutes, with no other filter. The only profitable combinations are enter every 60m / hold 45m and enter every 30m / hold 45m. Everything else loses money — which shows that timing matters more than frequency. The Entry Clock bar at the top of the page shows your current UTC time and whether you're in a good or bad window right now.
