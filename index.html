Key finding: BTC has a persistent downward micro-bias ŌĆö after any sequence of flat bars, price breaks down ~1.2├Ś more often than it breaks up. Signals & Horizons ŌĆö What Is a Signal?
A signal is generated when BTC sits flat for 8+ consecutive minutes and then breaks out with a significant price move accompanied by elevated volume. The system places a LONG (betting price goes up) or SHORT (betting price goes down) based on the direction of the breakout.

The hit rate measures how often the signal was correct at various time horizons ŌĆö e.g. H3 means "was the call right 3 minutes later?", H10 means "10 minutes later". A HIT means price moved in the predicted direction, a MISS means it moved the other way, a TIE means price was flat.

Key finding: SHORT signals dramatically outperform LONG signals (49.2% vs 29.2% hit rate at H10). This aligns with BTC's persistent downward micro-bias shown on the Pattern tab. The BTC Horizon Forecast shows the model's directional probability for the next 1 minute through 24 hours.

Model Explainer ŌĆö How Does the Engine Work?
The RRP TECH engine runs a fully automated 5-step pipeline once a day. Here's what each step does in plain English:

1. Data Collection ŌĆö Downloads every 1-minute BTC price candle from Binance. Currently has 190,000+ candles going back to November 2025.
2. BTC Regime Engine ŌĆö Scores 6 macro signals (ETH/BTC ratio, 3-day momentum, order book imbalance, Fear & Greed index, hashrate change, USDT flows) each on a ŌłÆ1/0/+1 scale. Total score determines if we're in a Bull, Bear, or Neutral regime. This acts as the master gate ŌĆö if BTC is bearish, no buys are made.
3. BTC Prediction Engine ŌĆö A Random Forest (an ensemble of 800 decision trees) trained on 89 technical features predicts BTC's direction at 7 different time horizons (1 min to 24 hours).
4. Sniper Scanner ŌĆö Ranks the top 15 liquid coins by a composite momentum + volume score called TopMoverScore. Each coin gets a direction label (UP/NOUP) from a second logistic regression model.
5. Alpha Dashboard ŌĆö Combines regime + model confidence to assign an allocation tier (STRONG_BUY ŌåÆ SKIP) and a Kelly Criterion position size (a mathematically optimal bet-sizing formula that risks more when confidence is higher).

AUC = Area Under Curve ŌĆö the model's ability to distinguish UP from DOWN coins. 0.5 = coin flip, 1.0 = perfect. Brier Score = how calibrated the probabilities are ŌĆö lower is better. Val Recall = out of all the actual UP coins, how many did the model correctly identify.

Acorn Model Health ŌĆö Is the AI Getting Better?
The sniper model (a Logistic Regression classifier) retrains itself every time the pipeline runs, adding the latest batch of trades to its training data. This tab tracks whether the model is actually improving over time.

AUC (Area Under Curve) is the headline accuracy metric ŌĆö think of it as a score from 0 to 1 measuring how well the model separates winning coins from losing ones. Below 0.5 = worse than random, 0.5ŌĆō0.65 = weak edge, above 0.65 = meaningful edge. The chart shows every training run so you can see the model's journey.

Brier Score measures probability calibration ŌĆö when the model says "60% chance of going up", does it actually go up 60% of the time? Lower Brier = more trustworthy probabilities. This is the most consistently improving metric ŌĆö down from 0.46 early February to 0.243 now.

Recall = out of all coins that actually went UP, what % did the model correctly flag? Precision = out of all the coins the model flagged as UP, what % actually went UP. High recall with lower precision means the model casts a wide net ŌĆö it catches most winners but also some losers. Feature importance shows which signals the model is leaning on most ŌĆö liquidity (median_minute_usd) now dominates, which may be a concern as the model grows.
